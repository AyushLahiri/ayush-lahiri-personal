About Me

Hi! I’m Ayush. I’m a data scientist fascinated by the intersection of development economics, public policy, and computational methods, obsessed with economic measurement in low- and middle-income countries.

I enjoy torturing myself thinking about measuring the unmeasurable (or is it?) — using satellite imagery, Call Data Records, digital traces, and NLP to help people with the tools I have (currently limited to a sort of fancy keyboard).

Currently, I work as a Data Scientist at HarrisX in Washington D.C., where I build ML pipelines for large-scale surveys for clients in International Development, Politics, and the Private sector.

Working Papers & Research

Does Caste Homogeneity Enhance Visible Development? The Disconnect Between Reported Expenditure and Nighttime Lights in Rural India
with B. Holland & H. Kumar • Working Paper (Economics), 2024

We test the efficacy of fiscal decentralization by asking if reported public spending translates into visible development (VIIRS nighttime luminosity) in 5,842 Gram Panchayats (Odisha). We find that changes in reported expenditure had almost no predictive power for changes in luminosity, a disconnect that persists regardless of the ethnic homogeneity of the local council.

Predicting Urban Heat Island Intensity via Computer Vision and Spatial Feature Distribution
Working Paper (Geospatial/CV), 2024

A novel methodology utilizing Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to analyze the visual spatial distribution of urban forms (buildings, streets, vegetation) to predict UHI intensity. This approach moves beyond aggregated metrics to provide a preemptive tool for urban planners.

Explaining Public Park Reviews in California using Natural Language Processing
with H. Kumar • Massive Data Institute: Greenspace Challenge, 2023

We use NLP to measure the lived experience of public greenspaces. Implemented a two-stage pipeline (LDA + DeBERTa-based Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis) on Google Reviews. Found a divergence between equitable spatial access and deficits in perceived quality (safety/maintenance) in lower-income tracts.

Methodological Projects

Ideology Analysis of Indian Supreme Court Judges
Advanced NLP / Computational Law

An attempt to ideologically scale Indian Supreme Court judges using judgement texts, focusing on government-involved cases. The methodology involved comparing STM, BERTopic, and GPT-4 Zero-shot topic modeling for case classification, followed by Doc2Vec with dimensionality reduction for ideological scaling.

Predicting Gentrification in DC
Unsupervised Clustering / Causal Prediction

Used unsupervised clustering on census variables (2012–2021) to create a bias-avoiding typology of gentrification in D.C. A Random Forest was then trained (using 2000 data and SMOTE oversampling) to predict 2031 gentrification probability, achieving 91% balanced testing accuracy.

Dashboard: Climate Change - Disparity in Responsibility and Impact
Visualization

A dynamic R-Shiny dashboard synthesizing 8 diverse datasets (NOAA, EM-DAT, PWT, Lucas Channel, etc.) to investigate the global disparity in climate change responsibility and impact. Demonstrating that high-income nations are the largest per capita contributors, while low-income nations exhibit significantly higher vulnerability and lower preparedness (ND-GAIN).
